WHY REGULAR AI FAILS IN AUTOMOTIVE

Engineers using off-the-shelf AI assistants face these critical breakdowns.

Your Intent Fades Over Time

The AI remembers your first message, but your original goal slowly drifts. After enough sessions, the agent is solving a different problem than the one you started with.

The Butterfly Effect

Each time the AI summarizes a chat, small details slip away. After 20+ sessions, those tiny omissions compound—and suddenly a minor note has become a core requirement, or a critical constraint has vanished entirely.

AI Invents Safety Rules

Standard AI confidently makes up automotive standards instead of citing real specs. In safety-critical systems, a hallucinated rule can lead to catastrophic failures.

Context Rot Over Time

As conversations grow, the AI compresses away the critical details. Your original intent slowly drifts until the AI is solving the wrong problem entirely.

Blind Code Changes

AI suggests changes without understanding what they break. One "small fix" can cascade through safety modules, sensor drivers, and control loops you didn't even know were connected.

No Domain Knowledge

Generic AI doesn't understand automotive-specific concepts: AUTOSAR layers, ASAM standards, timing constraints, or the difference between ASIL-A and ASIL-D requirements.

Dangerous Confidence

AI sounds certain even when it's completely wrong. Without provenance or citations, you can't tell if the answer came from real documentation or thin air.
